1) Market Time Cheat Sheet
High-probability windows
TIME SESSION WHAT INSTITUTIONS DO
2:00 – 5:00 AM London Open Set direction (build liquidity + define range)
8:00 – 10:00 AM NY Open Expand moves (deliver price to targets)
10:00 – 11:30 AM NY Continuation Final push (complete the delivery)
After 12:00 PM NY Lunch Distribution / trap zone (low quality entries)
Rule: If you trade outside these windows, you are usually trading against the main institutional flow.
2) Institutional Thinking (The Edge)
Mindset
Institutions do NOT

  • Chase breakouts

  • Trade randomly

  • Trade indicators alone

Institutions DO

  • Identify liquidity (where retail stops sit)

  • Manipulate price into that liquidity

  • Enter when retail exits

  • Hold through expansion (delivery)

Market = liquidity delivery system
Retail = liquidity provider
Price moves to orders
3) The 3-Phase Institutional Move
Model

  • Accumulation — Range, low volatility, “quiet” market

  • Manipulation — Fake breakout, stop hunts, liquidity grab

  • Expansion — Real move, strong displacement, targets hit

Common mistake: entering during Manipulation (phase 2).
Goal: enter after manipulation when expansion starts.
4) Daily Trading Routine (30–60 min)
Workflow

  • Night before (5–10 min)
    Mark yesterday high/low • Mark Asian range • Check next-day news

  • Pre-London (1:30–2:00 AM)
    Set bias (bull/bear) • Which side has more liquidity?

  • London Open (2:00–5:00 AM)
    Watch false breaks • No FOMO • Wait for manipulation

  • NY Open (8:00–10:00 AM)
    Main entry window • Trade with London direction • One clean trade only

  • Post-trade
    Journal outcome • Screenshot setup • Walk away

What looks wrong (fix this): most traders skip the routine, trade whenever they “feel like it,” and then blame the market. The routine is the edge.
5) News That Actually Matters
High-impact only

7) News Day Playbook
Liquidity event
Do NOT
Avoid

  • Trade 5 minutes before news

  • Chase the spike

  • Guess direction

Do
Process

  • Let the spike happen

  • Wait for retrace

  • Trade with higher-timeframe direction

News = liquidity event. The first move often grabs stops. The cleaner entry is usually after retrace and confirmation.
